📊 Key Findings

  • 65.4% of students spend more than 3 hours daily on social media.
  • Instagram is the most popular platform, used by 83.6% of respondents.
  • 60% of students continue using social media until 10 PM or later.
  • 54.5% of students sleep only 5–7 hours per night.
  • 52.7% reported that social media affects sleep quality.
  • 90.9% experience distraction while studying.
  • 45.5% believe excessive social media usage reduces productivity.
  • 67.3% have attempted to reduce screen time.

📝 Conclusion

The survey findings indicate that social media has a significant influence on students' daily lives. Increased social media usage is associated with reduced sleep quality, greater distractions during study sessions, and lower productivity levels.

💡 Recommendations

  • Limit social media use before bedtime.
  • Set daily screen-time limits.
  • Use focus mode while studying.
  • Take regular digital breaks.
  • Practice mindful social media usage.
